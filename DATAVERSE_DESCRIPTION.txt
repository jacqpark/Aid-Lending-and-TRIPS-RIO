------------------------------------------------------------

Replication package for "Aid, Lending, and TRIPS". The study
examines how the United States uses development finance to diffuse TRIPS-plus
intellectual property standards. It measures US trade pressure with a DeBERTa
stance-detection score built from National Trade Estimate reports. It then
estimates how bilateral aid in democracies and International Finance Corporation
lending in autocracies moderate the effect of that pressure on the number of
undisclosed information, patent, and enforcement provisions in partner
countries' preferential trade agreements. The package contains the analysis
script, an offline reproduction script, the merged analysis dataset, a saved R
workspace, and the regenerated tables and figures. Four third-party data files
are not re-hosted here because of redistribution terms or file size. Each is
documented with download instructions and a citation. See README.txt and
DATA_SOURCES.txt for full provenance and run instructions.
